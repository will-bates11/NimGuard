wbate@host:~$ ./nimguard_linux --trace --attach 4803 --trace-timeout 3000 --trace-max 20
[+] Attaching to PID 4803...
[+] Attached.
[+] Tracing syscalls (max 20 events, timeout 3000ms)...
    (no syscall events captured)
[+] Detached from PID 4803

Notes:
  - /tmp/traceable_sleep sits in a nanosleep loop. Over the 3-second window
    no syscall boundary is crossed that the tracer intercepts, so zero events
    are recorded.
  - To capture syscalls from a more active process (e.g. a server handling
    requests), substitute its PID and increase --trace-timeout.
  - The trace loop uses PTRACE_SYSCALL to step the target through kernel entry
    and exit points; each intercepted transition is recorded with its syscall
    number and argument registers.
  - WSL2 ptrace_scope=1: the target must either be a child of the tracer or
    have PR_SET_DUMPABLE set. /tmp/traceable_sleep satisfies the latter.

Platform: Ubuntu 24.04 WSL2, nimguard_linux x64
